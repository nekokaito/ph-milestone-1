My Travel Blog

During my visit to Sylhet, I had the incredible opportunity to spend quality time with my family, creating lasting memories in this enchanting city. Sylhet, with its lush green landscapes and picturesque tea gardens, provided the perfect backdrop for our family bonding. We explored the renowned Ratargul Swamp Forest, immersing ourselves in the serene beauty of nature. The vibrant Shahi Eidgah and Hazrat Shah Jalal University of Science and Technology added cultural richness to our experience. Savoring local delicacies, such as Sylheti biryani and tea, became a delightful culinary adventure. Things I brought from Sylhet

  1. Tea Sets: Exquisite and intricately designed, these tea sets beautifully reflect the rich tea culture of Sylhet.
  2. Sylheti Spices : Aromatic spices that add a touch of local flavor to my culinary endeavors, reminding me of the region's unique gastronomic delights.
  3. Handwoven Textiles: Traditional shawls and scarves, skillfully crafted and adorned with vibrant colors, serving as wearable mementos of Sylhet's artistic heritage.
  4. Local Artwork: Pieces of locally produced artwork capturing the essence of Sylhet's landscapes and cultural richness, adorning my home with a touch of the city's spirit.

My favourite food

  • Sylheti Biryani: Savor the unique flavors of Sylheti biryani, often made with aromatic rice, succulent meat (usually chicken or beef), and a blend of spices that set it apart from other biryanis.
  • Shatkora Beef Curry: This dish features beef cooked with shatkora, a tangy citrus fruit native to the region, creating a flavorful and zesty curry.
  • Mishti: Conclude your culinary journey with some Sylheti sweets, such as Sandesh, Roshogolla, or Chomchom, to satisfy your sweet tooth.
  • Fuchka/Pani Puri: Experience the local version of this popular street food, where hollow puris are filled with a spicy and tangy mixture, creating an explosion of flavors in each bite.
